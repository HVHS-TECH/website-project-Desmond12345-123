Explore the Parks ↓

Wellington's parks weave native bush, coastal views and quiet lawns into the fabric of the city. Whether you're after a brisk hill walk or a Sunday picnic, there's a green corner with your name on it.

01
Bush Walk

Khandallah Park

★★★★★ 4.5

Tucked behind the quiet suburb of Khandallah, this park is a true hidden gem. Dense native bush envelops a network of winding trails that reward you with glimpses of tūī, kererū and the distant harbour. The air smells of damp earth and kawakawa — a full sensory reset from the city below. Families love the playground near the entrance, while serious walkers can push further into the ridge for sweeping panoramas. Well-maintained paths and clear signage make navigation easy even for newcomers.

  • Native Bush
  • Birdlife
  • Playground
  • Dog Friendly
02
Regional

Belmont Regional Park

★★★★★ 4.8

Belmont is Wellington's great outdoor playground — vast, wild and endlessly varied. Stretching from the Hutt Valley foothills to the Porirua Harbour, it offers everything from gentle family strolls to challenging ridge routes with breathtaking views across Cook Strait. Mountain bikers, horse riders and trampers all coexist happily on its extensive trail network. The open tussock ridgelines feel genuinely remote, yet you're only minutes from suburbia.

  • Mountain Biking
  • Horse Riding
  • Panoramic Views
  • Tramping
03
Community

Kilbirnie Park

★★★★★ 4.0

Kilbirnie Park is the beating heart of the eastern suburbs — spacious, welcoming and almost always buzzing with activity. Wide open grass fields host weekend football matches and cricket games, while the skate park draws a loyal crowd. The recently upgraded play area is a big hit with little ones, and the proximity to the Evans Bay waterfront means a seaside stroll is always just moments away.

  • Sports Fields
  • Skate Park
  • Playground
  • Central Location
04
Landmark

Wellington Botanic Gardens

★★★★★ 4.9

Few places in New Zealand can match the sheer beauty and diversity of Wellington's Botanic Gardens. Established in 1868, this 26-hectare hillside sanctuary flows from the formal rose garden — glorious in summer — through towering native bush, glasshouse collections and the beloved Duck Pond. The cable car drops you right at the top, making the downhill wander into the city one of Wellington's finest experiences. Every season brings something new: tulips in spring, roses in December, golden tones in autumn.

  • Rose Garden
  • Cable Car Access
  • Native Bush
  • Glasshouses
  • Duck Pond
05
Family

Avalon Park

★★★★★ 4.3

Avalon Park in Lower Hutt is a family favourite that punches well above its weight. The park's centrepiece is a wonderfully nostalgic train ride that has delighted generations of Wellington children. Wide shaded lawns, barbecue areas and a well-equipped playground make it an ideal spot for a long lazy afternoon. The Hutt River runs nearby, adding a gentle soundtrack to picnics and afternoon naps.

  • Train Ride
  • BBQ Areas
  • Riverside
  • Playground
